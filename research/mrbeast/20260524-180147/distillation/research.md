## Design DNA

### Core

1. **One-glance premise**: the cover must communicate the video's entire click promise in under one second. A child should understand the basic conflict or reward.
2. **Oversized human focal point**: usually Jimmy's face or body is large, near the foreground, and emotionally readable. The face acts as the scale anchor.
3. **Extreme contrast pair**: many covers show cheap vs expensive, safe vs dangerous, poor vs luxury, old vs young, team A vs team B, or early day vs late day.
4. **Physical stakes made visible**: money, days survived, dangerous environment, giant prize object, or direct competition appears as a literal visual object, not an abstract mood.
5. **Minimal text with huge typography**: when text appears, it is usually a short label like `$1`, `$100M`, `$1B`, or `DAY 99`. The words are not explanatory paragraphs.
6. **High-saturation commercial realism**: images look like polished composite thumbnails with crisp cutouts, clean lighting, strong contrast, bright blue/gold/red accents, and simplified backgrounds.
7. **Thumbnail-title unity**: title and image are designed together. The image does not repeat the full title; it completes or compresses it.

### Conditional

- Use split-screen when the topic is a comparison or transformation.
- Use a progress marker such as `DAY 97` when the topic is survival/endurance.
- Use large crowd geometry when the topic is mass competition.
- Use a dream prize environment when the topic is "win X" or "I bought X".
- Use red/blue team colors when the concept is team-vs-team.

### Weak

- Jimmy often smiles in aspirational/luxury covers and looks stressed in survival covers, but the expression is context-dependent.
- Gold objects often signal luxury, but the deeper rule is "the expensive side must be immediately more desirable."
- Some newer covers use no text at all when the visual premise is already obvious.

## Composition Rules

- Default aspect ratio: YouTube 16:9.
- Make one focal hierarchy: face first, premise object second, text third.
- Put the human face large enough to read at mobile size; crop near head-and-shoulders or upper body unless a full-body survival scene is necessary.
- Use a strong three-zone layout for comparisons: bad side, face, good side.
- For survival thumbnails, use a single immersive environment plus one obvious rule marker: circle, raft, bunker, storm, timer, or day count.
- For group competition thumbnails, use converging lines of people or mirrored teams to create scale and conflict.
- Reserve corners for short labels; never bury text inside visual noise.
- Avoid more than three major visual ideas.

## Subject Rules

- The subject must be concrete: person, money, timer, prize, danger, object, or group.
- If the user wants their own face, require a reference image. Do not invent their likeness.
- Do not use MrBeast's likeness in the generated cover unless explicitly authorized; translate his role into "the user's own host/creator portrait."
- The host should visibly react to the premise: excited for reward/status, strained for survival, shocked for danger, confident for comparison.
- Prize objects should be enlarged or staged so their value is obvious even without title text.

## Text Rules

- Prefer 0-4 words or one numeric label.
- Use thick, bold, sans-serif uppercase text.
- Common label types: `$1`, `$1M`, `$100M`, `$1B`, `DAY 6`, `DAY 99`.
- Use white fill with heavy black stroke for maximum contrast, or plain white on clean dark/blue backgrounds.
- Do not put the full video title on the image.
- Text should add the missing variable, not explain the whole premise.

## Color And Lighting Rules

- Use saturated blues, clean whites, bright reds, and gold/yellow luxury accents.
- Push value contrast: dirty/gray side vs clean/bright side; storm/danger vs luxury/safety; red team vs blue team.
- Use crisp composite lighting with strong face clarity.
- Backgrounds should be simplified enough to read on a phone.
- Expensive/reward elements should be brighter, cleaner, and more saturated than cheap/danger elements.

## Hook Mechanics

- **Price ladder**: show the two extremes, not every step. The viewer should immediately see "from nothing to absurd luxury."
- **Endurance countdown**: show late-stage progress and visible strain. `DAY 97` is stronger than "100 days" because it implies near-failure and near-victory.
- **Mass competition**: show the quantity through rows, crowds, or opposing teams; make the contest rule visible.
- **Prize fantasy**: show the prize as a physical dream object or environment.
- **Danger/survival**: show a simplified danger symbol plus the human cost on the subject's body or expression.

## GPT Image 2 Prompt Rules

- Do not say "make it in MrBeast's exact style." Instead, specify the transferable rules: large expressive host, clear extreme contrast, one-sentence click promise, saturated commercial thumbnail, minimal text, phone-readable hierarchy.
- Always specify `finished YouTube thumbnail, 16:9`.
- State exact on-image text once.
- If using a face, say "use the provided reference image of the creator as the host."
- If no face reference is provided, use a generic original host or focus on objects/scenes.
- For comparison videos, prompt a split-screen or three-zone layout.
- For survival videos, prompt one dramatic environment plus one day/progress label.
- For competition videos, prompt opposing groups or crowd scale.
- Require no extra text, no copied logos, no watermarks, and no exact recreation of existing MrBeast thumbnails.

## Avoid

- Do not copy MrBeast's face, clothing, logo, or exact thumbnail layouts.
- Do not use Squid Game symbols, branded properties, or specific IP unless the user has rights.
- Do not create cluttered collages with too many objects.
- Do not use small subtitle-like text.
- Do not use generic cinematic darkness that hides the premise.
- Do not create a beautiful poster when the goal is a clickable thumbnail.
- Do not make the expensive/prize side subtle; it should be obvious.

## User Input Questions

Ask only for missing production facts:

1. What is the video topic or working title?
2. What exact on-cover text should appear, if any?
3. Which template fits the idea: price comparison, survival/endurance, group competition, prize fantasy, or other?
4. What platform/aspect ratio is required?
5. Should the user's own face appear? If yes, ask for a reference image.
6. What must appear in the image?
7. What must not appear?
8. Are there brand colors, logos, or legal constraints?
