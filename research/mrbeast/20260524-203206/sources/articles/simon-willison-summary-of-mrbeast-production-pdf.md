Key evidence:

- Willison highlights that the creative process starts with title and thumbnail.
- Title and thumbnail define the expectations that the video must satisfy.
- The example comparing "front yard" with "ketchup" supports the principle that physical extremity is more clickable than ordinary setting.
- The first minute must prove to the viewer that the title/thumbnail promise will be delivered.
- The "wow factor" is a production and packaging device: a surprising physical thing that few others can do.
- Critical components are the elements without which the video promise collapses.

Implication for distillation:

MrBeast's generation engine is a stakes-and-proof engine: the cover must show the extreme premise and the proof object, not merely decorate the topic.
